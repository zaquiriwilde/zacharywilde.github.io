What I Believe

Convictions that
shape how I lead.

i.
Theatre is community infrastructure. Not a luxury. A place where a city sees itself honestly and grapples with what it wants to become.
ii.
Artists deserve space to fail. The best work comes from rooms where risk is welcomed, not penalized.
iii.
The business side is an artistic act. Budgets, boards, and fundraising aren't separate from the mission — they're how you protect it.
iv.
Equity isn't a checkbox. It shows up in who you hire, what you program, what tickets cost, and who's in the room when decisions get made.
v.
Development is relationship, not transaction. Donors and funders give to people and communities they trust. Building that trust is a core leadership responsibility.

On Innovation

My Stance
on AI in Theatre

"AI is a tool, not a replacement. Its value depends entirely on the intention and humanity behind it."
→
Excited, not naiveAI can deepen storytelling and create performance moments that weren't previously possible. That's worth exploring seriously.
→
Human first, alwaysThe artist is the point. Technology serves the work — never the other way around.
→
The environmental cost is realTraining one AI model can consume 700,000+ liters of water. Innovation without environmental accountability isn't leadership — it's novelty.
→
Revenue innovationAI-generated scenic design reproduced as miniature merch. New revenue streams that connect audiences to the work beyond the performance itself.
→
My approachExperiment boldly. Implement thoughtfully. Always ask: who benefits, and who bears the cost?

Why Regional Theatre
"I didn't choose regional theatre.

I grew up in it.

My first stage was a children's theatre. My second was a community theatre. By the time I found my way to regional work, I understood something that can't really be taught — that theatre doesn't exist in spite of its community. It exists because of it.

I've never had an interest in any other world. The regional theatre is where I believe the most honest, most necessary work gets made.

Regional theatre isn't where I'm starting. It's where I belong."
